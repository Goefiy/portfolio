Jade's

In the Vormgeving course, I learned to research and experiment to achieve appropriate visual designs. Working with a fixed inspiration and someone else's style was a pleasant challenge. I also developed new skills for designing form, color, and typography. This was important because my assigned designer was a typographer and graphic designer herself. Through constructive feedback and weekly iterations, I achieved a satisfactory final result.

The Assignment, The Inspiration, The Expo

"Design a single page website for a restaurant. The single page consists solely of shape, color, and text, so no images. The style of a Dutch designer is the starting point for the design."

Susanne Heynemann was a Dutch typographer and graphic designer of German origin. She became known for book covers she designed for authors like Dola de Jong and Hella Haasse at Querido publishing house. Later, she became responsible for the design of textbooks from Wolters publishing house. Heynemann is seen as one of the most versatile and productive typographers in the Netherlands.

At the Design Expo, we got together with other designers from different classes, some had the same inspiration as myself. It was interesting to see how others interpreted Susanne Heynemann and made her work their own. The Expo also included a presentation of your work and an explanation of your iterations and design choices, 2 teachers from the Vormgeving course were present and asked questions.
